Our Jewellery Making Process

From design concept to finished heirloom — how Payal & Minal Gems craft quality jewellery

Overview — how a piece is made

Creating fine jewellery is a multi-disciplinary process combining artistry, metallurgy, gemology and precision engineering. We follow a robust process: Concept → Design → Prototype → Manufacture (casting/fabrication) → Stone setting → Polishing & finishing → Quality control & certification → Packaging & delivery. Below are the details of each step, why they matter and what we do to ensure beauty, durability and traceability.

Typical timeline

Ready designs: 3–10 days (stock pieces)

Custom orders: 2–6 weeks depending on complexity

Repairs & resizing: 3–10 days

Timelines vary with material availability, gemstone sourcing and certification requirements.

Step-by-step — the full process

  • 1) Concept & Design

    Everything starts with an idea — a sketch, mood board or customer's brief. Designers translate concepts into technical sketches, deciding proportions, sizes and gem placements. At this stage we select materials (karat, metal color), gemstone specifications and initial cost estimates.

  • 2) CAD / 3D Modelling

    Designs are modelled in CAD (Computer-Aided Design) software. CAD allows precise control over dimensions, prong geometry and stone seats. We create photorealistic renders so customers can visualise the finished piece and approve changes before prototyping.

  • 3) Prototyping (Wax / 3D Print)

    We produce a prototype using traditional hand-carved wax or modern 3D printing. Prototypes allow a tactile check of fit, scale and comfort and are essential for complex designs and custom commissions.

  • 4) Casting (Lost Wax)

    The approved wax model is invested in plaster, burnt out, and molten metal (gold/other alloy) is cast into the cavity. Casting yields accurate reproductions and is efficient for both single pieces and small batches.

  • 5) Fabrication & Hand-finishing

    For bespoke and high-end items, skilled goldsmiths fabricate parts by hand — cutting, forming, soldering, and fitting. Hand-finishing provides superior strength and a refined surface that machine-only processes often cannot match.

  • 6) Stone Setting

    Gemstone setting is a precision task: prongs are shaped, bezels are formed, and pavé stones are micro-set. Setting methods (prong, bezel, channel, pavé, tension) are selected for security and aesthetics. Every setting is inspected to ensure stones are secure.

  • 7) Polishing & Surface Finish

    Polishing brings the metal surface to a mirror finish or desired texture (satin, brushed). White gold pieces are rhodium-plated for a bright finish. Surface treatments are selected to match design intent and durability requirements.

  • 8) Quality Control & Hallmarking

    Each finished piece is inspected for dimensions, stone security, symmetry, polish and weight. Jewelry above threshold purity is hallmarked and documented. We provide certificates and invoices detailing metal content, gemstones and care instructions.

  • 9) Packaging & Delivery

    Pieces are carefully cleaned, packaged in branded boxes, accompanied by certificates, warranties and care instructions. For shipments, we use insured courier services and provide tracking and delivery confirmation.

Design — from sketch to CAD

Design balances aesthetics with engineering. CAD provides exact tolerances for prongs, setting seats and band thickness. We simulate stresses and check that the design will wear well in real life (e.g., adequate metal thickness at stress points).

  • Photorealistic renders for approval.
  • Dimensioned drawings for workshop reference.
  • Material selection and cost estimation integrated into the design stage.

Prototype methods

We use both hand-carved wax and high-resolution resin 3D prints. 3D prints are faster for complex geometry; wax allows fine texture control. Prototypes also let clients try on a model to refine comfort and scale.

Casting vs Fabrication — when we use each

Casting (best for)

Complex, sculptural shapes; efficient small-batch production; faithful reproduction of CAD geometry.

Pros: Accurate detail, quicker for multiple copies. Cons: Requires finishing to remove sprues and vents; porosity risk if not controlled.

Fabrication (best for)

Bespoke one-off pieces, heavy-duty items, heirloom restorations and designs needing hand finishing.

Pros: Strong joins, artisanal control, excellent for unique pieces. Cons: Labour-intensive and higher making charges typically.

Quality control & certification

Quality is non-negotiable. Our QC includes:

  • Dimensional checks and weight verification
  • Stone security (tug & visual tests)
  • Surface finish inspection (polish, plating uniformity)
  • Hallmarking and purity verification (XRF, assay when required)
  • Certificate issuance for diamonds and major gemstones
Documentation provided
  • Tax invoice with metal weight, purity & making charges
  • Gemstone certificates (GIA/IGI/AGS etc.) where applicable
  • Warranty card and care instructions

Sustainability, conflict-free sourcing & traceability

We source responsibly: lab-certified diamonds or traceable supplier chains, recycled gold where possible, and ethical refiners. For high-value purchases we maintain chain-of-custody records and can provide supplier documentation on request.

If sustainability is a priority, ask about recycled-metal options and lab-grown gemstone alternatives — both reduce environmental footprint and are fully documented.

How pricing works

Jewellery price = Metal value (weight × spot price adjusted for purity) + Gemstone value + Making charges (labour, finishing) + Taxes + Any certification or assay fees. For custom pieces additional costs include prototyping and special finishing.

Metal pricingCalculated from daily spot price and karat conversion (e.g., 18K = 75% gold × spot price).
Making chargesDepends on design complexity, fabrication method and hand-finishing time.
Certificate feesIndependent certification (e.g., GIA) is an additional cost for gemstones.
